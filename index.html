Magee College, Intelligent Systems Research Centre, Ulster University

About me

I did my undergraduate in mathematics in Trinity College Dublin. After this I taught Mathematics, Further Mathematics, and Physics for a short while, before returning to academia to complete a further Masters in Research and then begin a PhD in Mathematical and Computational Neuroscience.

I am also involved in a visiting research collaboration at Bristol University.

Research

As for my research, see here for more detail, but I have an interest in synaptic plasticity, spectral and dynamic graph theory, reservoir networks, and brain connectome data.

  • poster- International Conference of Mathematical Neuroscience, Dublin, June 2024: "Heterosynaptic plasticity rules induce small-world network topologies"
  • poster - UK Neural Computation Conference, Sheffield, July 2024: "Brain connectome-inspired network connectivity for reservoir computing"


Group

I am in the O'Donnell Group computational neuroscience research group, split between Magee College, Ulster University, Northern Ireland, and University of Bristol, in south-west England. This group is also linked with the Houghton group - another computational neuroscience group which is based in the University of Bristol.
